## This Guide Might NOT Be Right If...

Let's be honest about limitations:

### ‚ùå You Want Deep Expertise in One Tool

**The issue:**

This guide introduces many tools but doesn't deeply cover any single one. If you want to become a Pandas expert or master Spark, you'll need specialised courses.

**Alternative:**

Use this guide to identify which role interests you, then pursue tool-specific courses for that role.

---

### ‚ùå You Want Video Tutorials

**The issue:**

This guide is entirely text and code-based. There are no video walkthroughs.

**Why:**

Written tutorials are more accessible, easier to follow at your own pace, and better for quick reference. But if you strongly prefer video learning, this might not suit your style.

---

### ‚ùå You Need Immediate Job Placement

**The issue:**

This guide doesn't guarantee job placement or provide direct employer connections. It's an educational resource, not a recruitment service.

**What it does provide:**

Skills, knowledge, and portfolio projects that will help you in job searches‚Äîbut you'll need to apply and interview independently.

---

### ‚ùå You're Only Interested in One Specific Role

**The issue:**

This guide's strength is breadth‚Äîshowing you many roles. If you're 100% certain you want to be a Data Scientist and nothing else, you might prefer a specialised Data Science course.

**However:**

Even if you're set on one role, understanding adjacent roles makes you a better collaborator and helps you verify that role is truly right for you.

---

### ‚ùå You Want Quick, Surface-Level Learning

**The issue:**

The hands-on projects require real time investment. You'll write actual code, build actual systems, and troubleshoot actual errors.

**Time commitment:**

- Reading all role pages: 3-4 hours
- Complete hands-on project: 15-20 hours
- If you're not willing to invest that time, you won't get the full benefit

## Common Questions

### "I'm not very technical‚Äîis this too advanced?"

**No!** We assume zero prior coding experience. We teach you everything you need, step-by-step. Yes, you'll feel confused at times‚Äîthat's normal and expected. Learning data work means getting comfortable with temporary confusion.

### "I'm already a developer‚Äîwill this be too basic?"

**Parts will be.** You'll breeze through setup and basic Python. But you'll still learn data-specific concepts, tools (Airflow, dbt, Snowflake), and how data teams operate. Focus on the new-to-you sections.

### "I don't have much time‚Äîcan I do this in bits?"

**Absolutely.** Each tutorial is self-contained. Do one per week if that suits you. The knowledge is cumulative but you can take breaks between sections.

### "Do I need a maths or statistics background?"

**Not required.** We explain statistical concepts when needed, and the ML sections use beginner-friendly algorithms. If you're comfortable with basic algebra (which we rarely use), you're fine.

### "Will this help me get a job?"

**It will help, but it's not sufficient alone.** You'll gain:

- Understanding of roles (helps target applications)
- Practical skills (helps pass interviews)
- Portfolio projects (helps stand out)
- Knowledge of tools (helps meet job requirements)

But you'll still need to apply to jobs, network, prepare for interviews, and potentially do additional learning for your target role.

### "Is this guide kept up-to-date?"

**Yes, it's actively maintained.** Data tools and roles evolve, so this guide is updated regularly. It's also open-source‚Äîif you spot outdated info, you can suggest updates.

### "Can I do this on Windows? Mac? Linux?"

**All three work.** Instructions are provided for all major operating systems. Some commands differ slightly, but we note those differences.
